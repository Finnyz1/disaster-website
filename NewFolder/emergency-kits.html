Emergency Kits

Having a well-stocked emergency kit can make all the difference during disasters. Prepare now to stay safe!

Why Do You Need an Emergency Kit?

An emergency kit ensures that you and your family have essential supplies during disasters, power outages, or evacuations. It can help sustain you until help arrives.

What Should Be in Your Kit?

  • Water: At least 3 liters per person per day.
  • Food: Non-perishable items like canned goods, protein bars, and dried fruits.
  • First Aid Kit: Bandages, antiseptic, pain relievers, and prescription medications.
  • Flashlight & Batteries: Ensure you have extra batteries or a hand-crank flashlight.
  • Radio: A battery-powered or hand-crank radio to receive emergency updates.
  • Multi-tool: A Swiss Army knife or similar tool for various needs.
  • Personal Documents: Copies of ID, insurance, and emergency contact info.
  • Warm Clothing: Extra clothes, blankets, and rain gear.
  • Cash: Small bills in case ATMs and card readers are unavailable.

Maintaining Your Emergency Kit

Check your emergency kit every six months. Replace expired food, water, and batteries. Update your documents and adjust supplies based on your family's needs.
